Noto Sans Nandinagari is a design for the historical Indic _Nandinagari_ script.

Noto Sans Nandinagari contains 676 glyphs, 20 OpenType features, and supports 92 characters from the Unicode block Nandinagari.

### Supported writing systems

#### Nandinagari

Nandinagari (𑧁𑧞𑧤𑦿𑧁𑧑𑦰𑧈𑧓) is a historical Indic abugida, written left-to-right, with unconnected headstrokes. Was used in the 8th–19th centuries in South India for Sanskrit texts about philosophy, science and the arts. Closely related to Devanagari. Read more on [ScriptSource](https://scriptsource.org/scr/Nand), [Unicode](https://www.unicode.org/versions/Unicode15.0.0/ch15.pdf#G106413), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO_15924:Nand), [Wiktionary](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Category:Nandinagari_script), [r12a](https://r12a.github.io/scripts/links?iso=Nand).
