Noto Sans N’Ko is an unmodulated (“sans serif”) design for texts in the African _N’Ko_ script.

Noto Sans N’Ko contains 184 glyphs, 5 OpenType features, and supports 79 characters from 2 Unicode blocks: N’Ko, Arabic.

### Supported writing systems

#### N’Ko

N’Ko (ߒߞߏ) is an African alphabet, written right-to-left. Used in West Africa for the Manding languages. Created in 1949 by Solomana Kante. The name of the script means “I say”. Has 19 consonants, 7 vowels and 8 diacritics. Influenced by the Arabic script.
