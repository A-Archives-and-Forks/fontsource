British visual artist and philosopher [Shantell Martin](https://shantellmartin.art/) is famous for using words in her artwork in the [Oculus](https://shantellmartin.art/work/the-oculus/) at the World Trade Center, in New York City, and for her [music and art collaboration with Kendrick Lamar](https://shantellmartin.art/work/x-kendrick-lamar/) at Art Basel in Miami. Her art has taken over the [screens of New York’s Times Square](https://observer.com/2020/02/shantell-martin-times-square-midnight-moment-billboards/) and the [Lincoln Center](https://shantellmartin.art/work/new-york-city-ballet/) stage, home of the New York City Ballet.

Back in school, she was scared of spelling tests. However, outside of school, she felt that words were art and provided emotional relief. A discovery in her early 20s opened her eyes to why reading and writing were so difficult for her, and set in motion her desire to create the [](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Shantell+Sans)To inspire others to have fun with writing and words, she teamed up with Stephen Nixon of [Arrow Type](https://arrowtype.com/) to create Shantell Sans.

Shantell Martin, Stephen Nixon, and Anya Danilova share their experiences of the making of Shantell Sans.
