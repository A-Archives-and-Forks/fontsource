The Ruslan Display font is based on a 1970s typeface made by Ukrainian designer Oleg Snarsky, which evokes the _ustav_ and _semiustav_ styles of the 11th–16th centuries, known as the [Ruthenian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruthenians) period. This is featured in the signage of Kiev's [Teremky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teremky_(Kyiv_Metro)) metro station, and in a collection of Snarsky's typefaces in the book “Шрифты-алфавиты для рекламных и декоративно-оформительских работ“ published in 1979 and [available online](https://my.etnoua.info/novyny/knyha-shryfty-alfavity-osnarskyj-1979/).

It was digitized and extended with an original Latin complement by Russian designer Denis Masharov, in collaboration with Vladimir Rabdu, in 2011. The name means [‘lion’](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruslan_(given_name)).

Suitable for lettering, Ruslan Display can also be used to set short texts and supports Latin and Cyrillic.
